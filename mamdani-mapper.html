A running receipt of every public stop the mayor has made, plus credibly pre-announced upcoming events. Past stops come from the NYC Mayor's Office press feed (~1–3 days after the event); upcoming stops come from a daily structured search of credible NYC news. We plot only what's been published — never a tweet, never an inference, never a third-party signal. When the press feed reads "Mayor visited La Marqueta in East Harlem", his face appears at La Marqueta.

§ 07 · the data desk · how this is made
read before you @ us
source
Every 5 minutes we pull new press releases from the NYC Mayor's Office sitemap. Each release goes through Claude Haiku 4.5 to extract a specific venue (with a regex fallback). Mapbox geocodes the venue using neighborhood and borough as hints. The result lands in a static events.json committed to the repo. A skip-cache means most runs do zero Claude calls — only fresh URLs are evaluated.
never
We do not infer location from tweets, photos, license plates, EZ-Pass, or anything that wasn't already in a press release. We do not aggregate his current position from third-party signals. We do not accept crowd submissions.
cadence
Refresh runs every 5 minutes. Most past events show up 1–3 days after they happen, when the press office posts the readout or transcript. The default view is the rolling last 7 days.
forward signal
Once a day, Claude (Haiku 4.5) runs a structured web search across NYC press (Gothamist, NY1, NY Times Metro, City & State, RPA press releases, etc.) for credibly pre-announced events. Found events show with a dashed cobalt ring on the map. Source URL + a verifying quote are stored with each one and shown in the popup.
limits
Mamdani's office (unlike prior administrations) doesn't publish forward-looking daily schedules. So we can't tell you where he'll be tomorrow at 2pm — only where he was yesterday. If that changes, this map updates automatically.
corrections
Spot a wrong venue or missing event? email me. All edits go through a manual override file with a public diff.

the mapper is a one-night project. one static page, one mapbox tile, one hand-written scraper, and a github action that commits the receipt to the repo once a day. built for sport.
