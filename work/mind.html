a behavioral operating system. turning uncertainty into confident decisions — quantifying user hesitation and dynamically deploying interface patterns to resolve it.

● FRAMEWORK · 03A · 2024 BEHAVIORAL STATUS: LIVE SIBLING OF /work/hcai
CtB = (Clarity + Trust + Motivation) − (Friction × 2) − Risk
CONFIDENCE-TO-BOOK · quantifies a user's readiness to commit

Friction carries 2× negative weight. That's the core insight — and the reason the formula doesn't reduce to "add more clarity." A clean checkout with a forgivable price still loses to a confusing one with a great deal, because the friction tax compounds at every step.

01 — THE PHYSICS OF DECISION MAKING

three forces.

The framework operationalizes three fundamental psychological forces. None of them are new — they're inherited from forty years of behavioral economics. What's new is encoding them as design tokens you can tune.

01
Cognitive Efficiency
Humans are cognitive misers. We gravitate toward defaults, simple comparisons, and pre-curated options.
DESIGN IMPLICATION
Curate the path. Don't just provide choices.
02
Loss Aversion
Fear of loss is 2–3× more powerful than the desire for gain. The pain of losing outweighs the pleasure of gaining.
DESIGN IMPLICATION
Frame around avoiding missed opportunities.
03
Social Orientation
In moments of uncertainty, humans look to the herd. Social proof resolves ambiguity faster than argument does.
DESIGN IMPLICATION
Social proof at the exact moment of hesitation.
02 — THE 5 PILLARS OF INFLUENCE

five questions.

Every pillar addresses a question the user is silently asking. Walk a funnel and listen for the one that isn't being answered — that's where the leak is.

01 · ORIENTATION
"Do I understand what this is and why it matters?"
Clarity Headers · Context Frames · Pricing Anchors · Feature Grids
02 · MOTIVATION
"Do I want this?"
Dynamic Social Proof · Scarcity Signals · Value Highlights · Emotional Content
03 · DECISION ARCHITECTURE
"Is this choice easy?"
Smart Defaults · Comparison Grids · Best-Value Labels · Progressive Commitment
04 · TRUST & REASSURANCE
"Do I feel safe committing?"
Risk-Reversal Guarantees · Transparency Blocks · Review Panels · Authority Signals
05 · ACTION
"Is now the right moment to act?"
Verb-First CTAs · Momentum Locks · Micro-Reassurance · Progress Indicators
03 — CODIFIED INTO TOKENS

behavior as a token.

The framework only works if it's not a slide deck. These tokens let developers adjust psychological intensity programmatically — same surface, different funnel depths get different tunings, and the design system stays the source of truth.

// Behavioral Token Definitions
// Adjust psychological intensity programmatically.

type UrgencyLevel = "high" | "medium" | "low";
type TrustLevel   = "verified_partner" | "standard";
type PriceAnchor  = "strike_through" | "comparative";
type CTAFraming   = "commit" | "risk_free" | "soft_save";

interface BehavioralTokens {
  // Color intensity + copy phrasing for scarcity
  "behavioral.urgency": UrgencyLevel;

  // Prominence of validation signals
  "behavioral.socialproof": 1 | 2 | 3 | 4;

  // Visual weight of guarantees by funnel depth
  "behavioral.trust": TrustLevel;

  // How value context is displayed
  "behavioral.price.anchor": PriceAnchor;

  // Linguistic framing of action buttons
  "behavioral.cta.framing": CTAFraming;
}

// Example: high-stakes checkout step
const checkoutTokens: BehavioralTokens = {
  "behavioral.urgency":      "high",
  "behavioral.socialproof":  4,
  "behavioral.trust":        "verified_partner",
  "behavioral.price.anchor": "strike_through",
  "behavioral.cta.framing":  "risk_free",
};
04 — BEHAVIORAL DROP-OFF INDEX

where the chain breaks.

BDI scores every step of a funnel against the four levers that move CtB. A leaking funnel is rarely all leaking — usually it's one step doing damage. The score finds the step.

BDI(step) = (Alignment + Motivation + Trust + (5 − Friction)) / 4
PER-STEP DIAGNOSTIC · 0 to 5, higher is healthier

reading the score

BDI ≥ 4.0
Strong alignment, low resistance. Don't redesign — instrument.
BDI 2.5 – 4.0
Optimization opportunity. The pillars to lift are visible in the sub-scores.
BDI ≤ 2.5
Behavioral leak, high abandon risk. Treat as a P0.

05 — THE ETHICAL ETHOS

four non-negotiables.

Ethics is not a constraint on behavioral design — it's a performance multiplier. Pressure tactics and false urgency lift conversion this quarter and tax retention every quarter after. The framework refuses them by construction.

Clarity over Persuasion

Users must understand the choice and its consequences. A confused yes is not a yes worth having.

Confidence over Pressure

Nudge toward certainty; reject false urgency. If the timer wouldn't be there with the customer in the room, don't put it on the screen.

Momentum over Friction

Accelerate through smart defaults, not dark patterns. Removing a click is design. Hiding a checkbox is not.

Integrity over Manipulation

Scarcity, social proof, and data must be real. If the "12 people viewing" is fake, the whole framework is fake.
