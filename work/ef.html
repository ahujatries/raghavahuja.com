CH. 01 — INSURANCE REDESIGN · LOCKED

★★★★★★★ ★★★★★★★ ★★★★★★★.

The insurance add-on was the highest-margin SKU on the platform with the lowest take-rate in category. Discovery surfaced a trust leak in the price moment: travelers were quoting the package without the add-on, then opening the add-on PDP only to find the same number with a $89 increase, no rationale, no context. The redesign moved disclosure upstream and reframed the moment as risk-reversal, not upsell.

I instrumented every step against the BDI heatmap — page-level Trust scores below 2.5 became the work. The design-system contribution was a new guarantee-block primitive that paired the policy summary with the cancellation window in a single vertical, with live exchange-rate computation on the deductible. Engineering shipped it behind a holdback; we ran a 3-week experiment with five geo-segments. Lift was statistically significant in 4 of 5; the one that didn't lift was the one with the highest pre-existing add-on rate, which was the right honest answer.

The takeaway was not the +10% itself — it was that the take-rate was held by the trust score, not the price, and once we instrumented that link the rest of the platform got a tool to find similar moments. Two adjacent surfaces — visa add-on, flexible-date upsell — were rebuilt the same way over the next quarter.
