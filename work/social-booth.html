I joined Social Booth in 2016 as one of the founding creatives. Over four years I built and led the design team from zero to 8+, and led campaign and launch creative for Ford India, Ducati India, and the Wendy's India launch — three major brand campaigns in the Indian market.

I left in 2020. Three years later, the agency was acquired by VaynerMedia and rebranded as VaynerMedia India.

WHAT I LED

four years, four pieces of work.

  • 01THE DESIGN TEAM (0 → 8+)
    Built design at Social Booth from a one-person creative function to an 8-person multidisciplinary team. Hired, mentored, ran creative reviews, set the studio's visual direction.
  • 02FORD INDIA
    Led campaign creative for Ford India during a high-stakes period of brand work in the Indian market.
  • 03DUCATI INDIA
    Led campaign creative for Ducati India — premium positioning work in a competitive premium-motorcycle segment.
  • 04WENDY'S INDIA LAUNCH
    Led launch creative for Wendy's entry into the Indian market. Launches are a different category of work from ongoing brand work — fixed dates, multi-stakeholder coordination, real commercial stakes from day one.
WHAT THIS TAUGHT ME

leadership is a different muscle.

Creative leadership is a different muscle than IC craft. Building a team teaches you how to give people work that grows them, how to evaluate output without doing the work yourself, and how to hold a studio's standards across a portfolio of clients with different needs. The brand work — Ford, Ducati, Wendy's — taught me how to operate when the stakeholder list is long and every piece ships under public scrutiny.

I left the agency in 2020. The work I did there is part of why VaynerMedia eventually acquired it — but that acquisition is their story, not mine to claim. What I claim is the four years of building, leading, and shipping that came before it.
